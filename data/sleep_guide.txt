Sleep Hygiene and Rest Improvement Guide

Why Sleep Matters
Chronic sleep deprivation increases cortisol, impairs memory consolidation, weakens the immune system, and worsens anxiety and depression symptoms. Adults need 7-9 hours per night.

Sleep Schedule Consistency
Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day including weekends. This regulates your circadian rhythm. Even one night of irregular sleep can disrupt your schedule for days.

Pre-Sleep Wind Down Routine
Start winding down 60 minutes before bed. Dim lights, avoid screens (blue light suppresses melatonin). Try reading a physical book, gentle stretching, or journaling. Keep your bedroom cool (65-68°F / 18-20°C).

Avoid These Before Bed
Caffeine: avoid after 2pm (half-life is 5-6 hours). Alcohol: disrupts REM sleep. Heavy meals: cause discomfort and elevate body temperature. Intense exercise within 2 hours of sleep.

When You Cannot Sleep
Do not lie in bed awake for more than 20 minutes. Get up and do a calm activity in dim light until you feel sleepy. This prevents the bed from becoming associated with wakefulness.

Stress and Sleep
Anxiety activates the fight-or-flight response, making sleep onset difficult. Writing down your worries before bed (brain dump journaling) reduces intrusive thoughts. Deep breathing lowers heart rate and prepares the body for sleep.
